What is Agile Scrum?
Scrum is a project management technique designed to provide a clear structure for activities, tasks and roles within the development progress of a desired future product (Product Vision). Scrum projects consist out of several specified timeframes, called sprints. The purpose of a scrum project is to develop a properly tested product enhancement at the end of every sprint and the technique allows for feedback during development. Teams using the agile scrum technique usually consist of seven people, who all have their own roles.
Roles in Agile Scrum

Product owner
The product owner represents the stakeholders of the project. They are responsible for re-prioritizing the Product-Backlog, adjusting expectations. They decide to (dis-)continue development and whether or not to release the product. The product owner is essentially the manager of the project.

Scrum master
The scrum master is the one who makes sure the scrum method is understood and adhered to. They make sure the team is not disturbed by inside or outside factors and provide an self-organizational environment.

Development Team members
A scrum development team is cross-functional and self-organizing, without assigned roles. Team members are extremely collaborative and in charge of developing the product enhancement.
