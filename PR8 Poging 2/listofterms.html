List of terms

Increment
The product enhancement you develop with Scrum Agile project management and which you intend to release at the end of a Sprint.

Sprint
A Sprint is a specified timeframe in which a product enhancement must be developed. A project consists of several sprints.

Product Backlog
The Product Backlog is the prioritized features list containing descriptions of every desired functionallity of a product.

Sprint Backlog
The Sprint Backlog is the complete project planning for the sprint, containg the Sprint Goal (why), the selected Product Backlog elements (what), and the plan for delivering the element (how).

Sprint Retrospective
During the Sprint Retrospective the team evaluates their process and decides what needs to be improved upon. It is essentially not only a project review, but also a peer-to-peer evaluation.

Sprint Burndown Chart
A Sprint Burndown Chart is a chart depicting the total amount of work remaining within the Sprint and is updated daily to keep track of the progress.

Burndown Velocity
The slope of the chart calculated by comparing the actaul hours worked to the estimated hours needed to complete the product.

Technical debt
'Code debt' or 'Design debt' are terms for the implied additional cost for rework. Usually technical debt is accumulated by choosing for an easier silution instead of an approach that would take longer, but would provide a better outcome.

Backlog Refinement
The process of refining the PBI so that every team member understands the upcoming Sprints entirely. This is a team activity.

Backlog Grooming
The process of reviewing the Product Backlog and ensuring that all elements and activities are properly prioritized.
